Try, try, try, again! And again. And again...

Monday, July 9th 2018

Wow! Today was tough! I have finished the Khan Academy Intro to JavaScript course, so I am doing some challenges on CodeWars to try and learn more and engage with JavaScript. Today I was working on a challenge to create a game called 'Pong.' The game involved a ball and two paddles, and the player moved their paddle to hit the ball. If a player missed the ball, their opponent got a point. It seemed like an easy enough challenge to complete, but I worked on it for HOURS! There were several moments when I almost gave up, but then a new idea would come to me and I would sit back down to try it. I finally came up with some code I thought might work, I hit the 'Run' button, and... errors! I extracted each piece of my code, tested it individually to see if it was working, found my errors, and all the sudden, BAM!, it worked! I was so excited I wooped, stood up and clapped! It was the best feeling! Definitely worth all the time I had put into the challenge to have that satisfaction. Here is the CodeWars challenge I was working on, and my solution.

I am very proud of my solution, but I will say it was hilarious when I saw the solutions of some other users! Their solutions were done in way, way less lines of code, using logic I don't know yet! They probably solved this problem in a matter of minutes. I am not disheartened though, I am encouraged! This just shows me that there is more capability to JavaScript that I have yet to learn!

GalvaNICE!

Monday, July 23rd 2018

I just got back from my first night of a course through Galvanize, and it was NICE! I am taking a JavaScript fundamentals course by Galvanize. Since I have been doing learning all on my own at home, I was anxious to see how a classroom environment would feel. I am happy to report that it was great! Having instructors and classmates learning alongside me was a real treat, if I get stuck (or should I say "when I get stuck"!) there are all these resources around me to help. Most of what we learned tonight, on the first night, I was pretty familiar with. But it was great to learn about it from another perspective!

The instructor, Wes, seemed very knowledgeable, helpful, and engaging. The class was two hours, and it flew by! I was particularly happy to see that Wes talked about the best ways of retaining new information. It is not by only listening, taking notes, etc; it is by DOING! I am very glad to see that Galvanize utilizes these principles. Being in this course, and enjoying it so much already, really makes me think that I would enjoy working as a programmer so, so much!
